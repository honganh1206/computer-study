16-bit number that's like the local address for the connection.

Used by the kernel to match an incoming packet to a certain process's socket descriptor.

> Analogy: IP address as the street address of a hotel, and port number as the room number.

Different services on the Internet have different port numbers, like HTTP with port 80 and SMTP with port 25.

> Ports below 1024 are reserved unless you are the superuser.
